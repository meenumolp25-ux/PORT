PM Vikas Program

Pradhan Mantri Virasat Ka Samvardhan — a flagship initiative for the holistic development of minority communities through skill development, education, and livelihood support.

Empowering Communities Through Inclusive Development

The PM VIKAS program consolidates the key interventions of previously existing schemes, focusing on skill training, education, and economic empowerment of minority communities across India. It aims to provide holistic development through a multi-sectoral approach.

Key Components

Day 1 — Orientation & Circuit Fundamentals

Introduced to basic circuit concepts: voltage, current, resistance, Ohm's Law, series/parallel networks. Lab demonstration clarified measurement techniques and safe handling of components.

Day 2 — Diodes & Rectifiers

Studied PN junction behavior, VI curves, and practical rectifier circuits. Built and observed half-wave and full-wave rectification and discussed filter capacitors' role.

Day 3 — Resistive Networks & Thevenin/Norton

Worked through equivalent circuits, Thevenin and Norton theorems, and practice problems simplifying complex resistive networks to compute load behavior.

Day 4 — Capacitors & Time Constants

Explored transient response of RC circuits, charging/discharging curves, and how time constants affect signal shaping and filtering.

Day 5 — Inductors & LC Behaviour

Focused on inductance, RL transients, and LC resonance. Built an LC tank circuit and observed frequency-dependent responses.

Day 6 — IoT Product Ideation

Brainstormed IoT project ideas, selected an initial smart-home integration concept, and sketched data flows and user scenarios for a prototype.

Day 7 — Semiconductor Basics

Reviewed carrier movement, doping, energy bands, and how material properties translate to device characteristics used in circuits.

Day 8 — Transistors: BJT & MOSFET

Compared BJT and MOSFET operation, biasing techniques, and small-signal models; set up simple amplifier stages on the bench.

Day 9 — Amplifiers & Gain

Measured gain, bandwidth, and input/output impedance of basic amplifier configurations; discussed trade-offs and stability concerns.

Day 10 — MOSFET Circuits & Switching

Examined MOSFET switching characteristics and designed driver stages for power switching and logic-level control.

Day 11 — Op-Amps & Applications

Explored operational amplifier configurations: inverting, non-inverting, summing, and filters; analyzed ideal vs real behaviours.

Day 12 — Analog Filters

Designed and simulated low-pass and high-pass filters, discussed cutoff frequency selection and real-component limitations.

Day 13 — Signal Analysis

Covered Fourier basics, frequency-domain intuition, and practical signal measurement techniques using the oscilloscope.

Day 14 — Digital Logic Basics

Introduced boolean algebra, logic gates, truth tables, and hazard considerations. Performed hands-on TTL/CMOS gate experiments.

Day 15 — Karnaugh Maps & Simplification

Practiced K-map simplification to minimize gate count and derived efficient combinational implementations for sample problems.

Day 16 — Combinational Circuits

Designed encoders, decoders, multiplexers, and arithmetic building blocks; discussed propagation delay and practical implementations.

Day 17 — Sequential Circuits

Covered latches, flip-flops, state machines, and timing diagrams. Implemented simple counters and state-based controllers.
